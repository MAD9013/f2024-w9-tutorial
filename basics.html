Basics

Container queries allow for another level of control when applying responsive CSS. Instead of using the size of our browser to determine the styles applied to our elements, we can reference the size of an ancestor of our element to determine when to apply different styles.

The provides us with more flexibility and reusability of our code. This could allow the same pattern to appear in two different areas of our site, but display different styles appropriate to the size of their referenced container without needing to change our code or add classes for each situation.
